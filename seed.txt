THE SECOND SON OF THE HOUSE OF BELLS

HOOK: In a city governed by tonal law -- where every contract, verdict,
and vow must be SUNG into validity -- a teenage boy with perfect pitch
discovers he can hear when someone is lying in a key that doesn't exist.

WORLD: Cantamura, a city built in a natural amphitheater where sound
behaves oddly -- whispers carry a quarter mile, shouts die three feet
from the speaker's mouth. Architecture accounts for this: government
buildings designed so defendants can't hear judges deliberate. Markets
have song-brokers who compose binding contracts in real time. Money is
tuned metal -- coins that ring at specific pitches, counterfeiting is
a musical offense. The Bellwright family maintains the city's 47
official bells, which set the civic pitch each morning. The family
workshop smells of metal-dust and linseed oil.

MAGIC SYSTEM (dual layer):
  SCIENTIFIC: Tonal Law. Specific intervals ratify, specific
  progressions bind, specific resolutions release. Learned at
  university, practiced with precision, deeply political.
  
  POETIC: What Cass hears is outside the system -- a dissonance in
  false oaths, a wrongness in songs sung under coercion. He can't
  prove it, teach it, or credential it. The false-note frequency
  causes him physical pain proportional to the stakes of the lie.

COST: The city runs on lies sung beautifully. Cass is in constant
low-grade agony in any public space. Every transaction, every
pleasantry, every polite evasion has a sound only he hears.

CHARACTERS:
  - Cass (14): brilliant, self-destructive. Training at the academy
    to master tonal law while his gift makes the system unbearable.
    Wound: his father knew about his brother's coerced contract and
    said nothing. Want: save his brother. Need: confront the truth
    that the system itself is the problem, not just one bad contract.
    Lie: "If I master the system, I can fix things from inside."
  
  - The brother (older): signed a binding contract he didn't
    understand. Now bound to service. The details of what he
    signed and why reveal themselves across the story.
  
  - The father: Bellwright patriarch. Hands that shake. Bells in the
    house Cass isn't allowed to touch. Knows more than he says.
    His silence is both protective and cowardly.

LORE DEPTH: The Bellwrights didn't just maintain the bells -- they
FOUNDED the system. The original Bellwright composed something into
the first bells 200 years ago that no one has been able to hear since.
Cass is starting to hear it. It isn't a law. It's a question. The
"true" history of Cantamura is always one layer deeper than what
anyone currently believes.

THEMES: When the system is built on a beautiful lie, is the person
who hears the truth brave or just broken? What do you do when the
thing destroying your family is the same thing that built your city?
Can you love an institution and oppose it at the same time?

TONE: Starts whimsical (the absurdity of singing your parking
disputes, musical counterfeiting, song-brokers haggling in the
market). Darkens as Cass discovers the depth of the coercion built
into the system. The humor never fully leaves -- it's how Cass
survives.
