## THE BELLWRIGHT FAMILY

---

### CASS BELLWRIGHT
**Age:** 14 | **Role:** Protagonist, POV character

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** When Cass was four, his brother Perin signed the Corda contract. Cass was not told what the contract said. He was not told it had happened. He pieced it together over months from his father's silence, his brother's absence, and the fact that the locked room off the kitchen acquired a new lock that winter. He has never been told he was right.

**Wound:** He was excluded from a crisis that destroyed his family, and no one has ever acknowledged the destruction. He lives in a house where the worst thing that happened is also the thing that cannot be named. This teaches him that his perception — what he hears, what he knows — is not to be trusted by others, even when it's correct.

**Lie:** *If I master the system well enough, I can fix things from inside it.*

**Want (external):** Free Perin from the Corda contract by finding the legal mechanism that voids it — a provable coercion, a lame chord, a flaw in the ratification.

**Need (internal):** Accept that the system has no mechanism for what happened to Perin because the system was designed to make coercion invisible. The fix is not a better argument. It is a different question.

**Truth (opposes the Lie):** *The system cannot fix what the system built.*

**Tension:** Cass's Want requires him to become more fluent in Tonal Law. His Need requires him to stop trusting it. Every step toward his Want moves him away from his Need, until the story forces the collision.

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 6 | He investigates, he pushes, he breaks rules — but he keeps redirecting his energy into the system rather than against it. His proactivity is real but misdirected until the third act. |
| **Likability** | 8 | He loves his brother, he's funny in a dry way, and he's in constant pain without self-pity. Readers root for him because he keeps going when he has every reason not to. |
| **Competence** | 7 | Genuinely gifted at tonal theory. His gift gives him information no one else has. But he's fourteen and keeps making the mistake of believing institutions will honor what's true. |

#### Arc Trajectory

Positive arc — disillusionment to agency. Starts believing the system can be reformed. Ends understanding that some things require you to step outside the system entirely. The arc is not cynicism — he doesn't stop loving Cantamura. He learns to hold love and opposition in the same hand.

#### Speech Pattern

1. **Vocabulary:** Technical musical terms as everyday language. "The interval's wrong" where another person would say "something's off." Anglo-Saxon concrete nouns. No abstractions unless he's being sarcastic.
2. **Sentence length:** Short when guarded. Longer when he trusts the person — the length of his sentences is a trust barometer.
3. **Contractions/formality:** Casual with Perin, formal-by-habit at the Academy, somewhere stripped and careful with his father.
4. **Verbal tics:** Asks "Which key?" when he means "what are you actually saying?" Uses "clean" as his highest compliment. Trails off mid-sentence when he hears an under-note — a pause that other characters read as distraction.
5. **Question ratio:** Higher than average. He's gathering information constantly. But his questions are specific, not open-ended.
6. **Interruption:** Rarely interrupts. When he does, it's because he's heard something that changes the conversation and can't wait.
7. **Metaphor domain:** Acoustic and material. "That argument has a lame chord in it." "He's tuned too tight." "The whole thing rang flat."
8. **Directness:** Indirect with emotion, direct with fact. He will tell you the exact pitch of a problem. He will not tell you it's hurting him.

**Example lines:**
- *"That fifth was flat. By a quarter-tone. Nobody flats a fifth on purpose."* (Observational. Sensory first, implication second. He reports what his ear caught.)
- *"She held the ratification for three seconds. You need four. So we don't have a contract. We have a gesture."* (Dry, specific, grounded in craft rules.)
- *"I know what I heard."* (Said quietly. Said once. Not repeated.)

#### Physical Appearance

Thin through the shoulders, still growing into his height. Dark hair that needs cutting, always. His hands are calloused from workshop work — not the soft hands of an Academy student. His left ear is slightly higher than his right, which his mother used to say meant he was always listening sideways. He has a habit of tilting his head when he hears an under-note, as if the angle might help him locate the source.

#### Physical Habits and Unconscious Tells

- **Tongue to the roof of his mouth** when he hears a lie — a habit learned from Perin, useless, kept anyway.
- **Left hand flat against his thigh** when the pain is bad. Pressing down. Grounding himself.
- **Goes still** in loud spaces — not relaxed still, held-breath still. The kind of stillness that takes effort.
- **Counts under his breath** in intervals when he's anxious. Not words. Pitch numbers.
- **Avoids eye contact** during conversations where he can hear an under-note. He doesn't want the other person to see that he knows.

#### Secrets

1. **The Founding Day silence.** On Founding Day, when all forty-seven bells ring, the under-note goes completely silent. He has heard this every year of his life and told no one. He doesn't know what it means. He's afraid of what it means.
2. **He's heard the harmonic in the Civic Pitch** — the thing beneath the sequence, older and lower than the bells themselves. He has been hearing it for six months. It is not painful. It feels like a question. He hasn't told his father because his father's hands shake worst on mornings when the Civic Pitch runs long, and Cass is afraid the two things are connected.

#### Key Relationships

- **Eddan (father):** Love expressed as shared work. Cass hands him tools. Eddan accepts them. Neither speaks about Perin. The silence is the relationship.
- **Perin (brother):** The organizing fact of his life. Perin taught him the tongue-to-roof trick, taught him to tune a bell by ear, taught him that the city's humor is how you survive its weight. Cass is trying to save someone who also shaped him.
- **Lenne (peer):** The first person at the Academy who asks him a question he can't answer with an interval. He doesn't know what to do with that.
- **Maret Corda:** He has never spoken to her. He has stood outside the Corda compound gate and listened to the bells inside ring at the wrong pitch for the district. He knows she holds his brother's contract. He hates her with the specific hatred of someone who has never had a target before.

#### Thematic Role

*When the system is built on a beautiful lie, is the person who hears the truth brave or just broken?*

### EDDAN BELLWRIGHT
**Age:** 52 | **Role:** Father, Bellwright patriarch

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** Ten years ago, Maret Corda came to the workshop with a contract already composed. Perin was sixteen. The Bellwright family owed a debt — not money, something older, a claim Maret held that traced back to the original bronze stock, to Aldric's journals, to the question of who actually owns the bells. Eddan was given a choice: Perin signs, or the Bellwright family loses custodianship of the forty-seven bells. He chose the bells. He has not stopped paying for it.

**Wound:** He traded his son for an institution. He tells himself it was to protect the family, the city, the system. The night of the signing, he read Aldric's journals for the first time — all of them. His hands began shaking within the week. He has never said whether the shaking comes from what he read or what he allowed to happen. Both explanations are probably true.

**Lie:** *Keeping the bells safe is the same as keeping the family safe.*

**Want:** Keep the system running long enough that Perin's contract expires naturally, without Cass ever learning what he did.

**Need:** Acknowledge that the bells are not worth what he paid for them — and that Cass already knows, and is waiting for his father to say it first.

**Truth:** *The family was the thing worth protecting. He protected the wrong thing.*

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 2 | He reacts, delays, retreats into work. His proactivity is entirely negative — he moves to prevent things from happening rather than to make things happen. |
| **Likability** | 5 | Readers understand him before they forgive him. He's not cruel. He's cowardly in the specific way of someone who loves too many things and chose wrong. |
| **Competence** | 9 | The best bell-wright in Cantamura. His hands shake at the table and settle at the lathe. His craft is the one place his guilt can't reach him. |

#### Arc Trajectory

Negative arc that breaks at the last possible moment — he deepens his paralysis until Cass forces the confrontation, at which point the arc becomes a question: can he act when it costs him the bells?

#### Speech Pattern

1. **Vocabulary:** Trade language. Tool names. Precise technical terms for bell-casting. Almost no emotional vocabulary — he has routed feeling into craft.
2. **Sentence length:** Short and instructional. "Hand me the pitch-gauge." "Strike it again." "That one's flat."
3. **Contractions/formality:** Informal in the workshop, almost wordless at the dinner table.
4. **Verbal tics:** Refers to bells by their district names rather than numbers. "The Seventh of Ash Street" not "Bell Forty-One." Each bell is a place, a relationship, a responsibility.
5. **Question ratio:** Very low. He gives instructions. He rarely asks because questions open conversations.
6. **Interruption:** Never interrupts. Waits. Sometimes the wait is long enough that the other person stops talking, which is what he was waiting for.
7. **Metaphor domain:** Material and structural. "That won't hold." "The alloy's wrong." Occasionally, about people: "He's cracked. Can't repair what's cracked through."
8. **Directness:** Direct about craft, completely indirect about everything else. His indirection isn't evasion — it's the grammar of a man who has learned that saying things aloud makes them real.

**Example lines:**
- *"The Seventh needs re-tuning before the quarter-feast. You can help if you've finished your academy work."*
- *"Your brother is well. He writes."* (He does not say when Perin last wrote.)
- *"Some bells can't be recast. You work with what you have."* (Said about bells. Not about bells.)

#### Physical Appearance

Broad through the chest, narrowing with age. Hands that are large and skilled and, since the Corda signing, unreliable — the tremor is worse in cold weather, worse at the dinner table, absent in the workshop. Gray at the temples. A burn scar on his right forearm from a casting accident when Cass was small. He has never explained how he got it. Cass has never asked.

#### Physical Habits and Unconscious Tells

- **The shaking hands settle when he works.** This is not a comfort — it's a tell. His body knows the workshop is the only place he doesn't have to be a father who failed.
- **Touches the wall between the kitchen and the locked room** when he passes it. Not a pat. A press. Brief. Checking that it's still there.
- **Hums while casting.** Not the Civic Pitch. Something older, lower. He doesn't know he does it.

#### Secrets

1. **The nature of the Corda claim.** Maret Corda holds a document — not a sung contract, a written one, old enough to predate the tonal system — that claims a share of custodianship over the original bells. It was signed by Aldric Bellwright's son, under circumstances Eddan has read about in the sealed journals and will not discuss. The document is legally unenforceable under Tonal Law. Maret knows this. She also knows Eddan doesn't want the Academy to examine it.
2. **He has read the journals.** All of them, the night of Perin's signing. He knows what Aldric composed into the first bells. He knows what the harmonic in the Civic Pitch is trying to say. His hands began shaking within the week — from the journals, from the signing, from the collision of both on the same night.

#### Key Relationships

- **Cass:** He sees Cass hearing things. He knows what it means. He is terrified of what Cass will do when he understands it fully. He loves his son with the helpless love of a man who has already failed one child and cannot afford to examine how.
- **Perin:** The wound that doesn't close. He reads Perin's letters in the workshop, alone, after Cass is in bed.
- **Maret Corda:** Ten years of cold transaction. He delivers bell maintenance reports to her compound quarterly. She accepts them without meeting his eyes. They have not spoken directly since the signing.

#### Thematic Role

*What do you do when the thing destroying your family is the same thing that built your city?*

### PERIN BELLWRIGHT
**Age:** 26 | **Role:** Absent brother, organizing absence

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** At sixteen, Perin discovered what the sealed journals contained — he found a loose page, misplaced, that described the harmonic in the Civic Pitch and what it meant. He went to his father. His father told him to put it back and never mention it. He couldn't. He started asking questions at the Academy. Maret Corda's people found him asking.

**Wound:** He tried to do the right thing and was bound for it. He has been in service to the House of Corda for ten years, composing contract music, tuning their resonance chambers, making their legal machinery run. He is very good at it. He hates that he's good at it.

**Lie:** *If I do the work well enough, they'll have no reason to keep me.*

**Want:** Finish the contract term and come home.

**Need:** Understand that the contract has no natural end — Maret Corda has built codicils into it that extend indefinitely, and the mechanism for proving this is the same mechanism that makes coercion invisible.

**Truth:** *Compliance is not a path out. It's a path further in.*

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 3 | He was proactive once and it cost him everything. He has learned to wait. This is the tragedy of his arc — the waiting is the trap. |
| **Likability** | 9 | He taught Cass everything gentle. He's funny, specific, kind in a way that costs him nothing because it comes naturally. Readers mourn him before they meet him. |
| **Competence** | 8 | Exceptional musical intelligence. The House of Corda keeps him because he's genuinely the best contract composer they have. His competence is the mechanism of his captivity. |

#### Presence Through Absence

Perin appears in the novel through:
- **Letters** — short, careful, stripped of anything that could be read as complaint. Cass reads the gaps.
- **Habits Cass carries** — the tongue-to-roof trick, the way Cass tunes by ear, the dry humor that survives everything.
- **What other characters say** — Eddan says "your brother is well" with the specific flatness of someone who has rehearsed it. Maret Corda's staff refer to him as "the Bellwright composer" without using his name.
- **The contract itself** — when Cass finally reads it, Perin's handwriting on the ratification line is the smallest thing on the page.

#### Speech Pattern (from letters and memory)

Perin writes the way someone speaks who has learned that words have consequences. Short sentences. Specific observations. Humor that arrives sideways. He never complains directly. He describes the weather, the quality of the Corda resonance chambers, what he had for lunch. The letters are full of what he doesn't say.

**Example (letter fragment):**
*"The resonance chamber on the east wing needs re-tuning. I've told them twice. They'll get around to it. The acoustics here are good otherwise — better than the Academy practice rooms, which I know isn't saying much. I hope father's hands are better. Tell him the Seventh of Ash Street always ran a quarter-flat in autumn. He'll know what to do."*

#### Secrets

1. **He knows about the harmonic.** He has known since he was sixteen. He has been composing around it for twelve years — small adjustments in the Corda contract music that leave a record, if you know where to look.
2. **He has been trying to reach Cass.** The letters contain something — a pattern in the specific intervals he describes, a message in the technical observations. He has been waiting for Cass to be old enough to hear it.

### MARET CORDA
**Age:** 60 | **Role:** Primary antagonist

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** Forty years ago, Maret was a song-broker's apprentice from the outer districts. She was talented and she knew it and the Academy rejected her application on the grounds that her folk-interval training was "acoustically uncodified." A merchant family took her on instead. She spent twenty years building the House of Corda from a mid-tier instrument supplier into the city's dominant legal infrastructure. She did it by understanding the system better than the people who designed it.

**Wound:** She was told she wasn't legitimate. She built legitimacy from scratch, and it still isn't the same thing as being recognized. The Academy has never invited her to lecture. The Civic Council has never offered her a seat. She holds more functional legal power than either institution and receives none of the recognition.

**Lie:** *Control is the same as security. If I hold enough contracts, nothing can touch me.*

**Want:** Maintain the Corda family's position as the indispensable center of Cantamura's legal economy — specifically, retain the Bellwright composer (Perin) whose work keeps their contract music at a level no competitor can match.

**Need:** Acknowledge that she built her empire on the same coercive mechanisms that destroyed her — that she became the thing that rejected her.

**Truth:** *You cannot build security on other people's captivity.*

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 9 | She is always three moves ahead. She doesn't react to threats — she composes them out of the music before they develop. |
| **Likability** | 4 | Understandable, not sympathetic. Readers can see exactly how she got here. That doesn't make her easier to like. |
| **Competence** | 10 | The most legally sophisticated person in Cantamura. She has never lost a contract dispute. This is not luck. |

#### Arc Trajectory

Flat arc — her tragedy is that she won't change. She is the mirror Cass is being held up to: what you become if you master the system instead of questioning it.

#### Speech Pattern

1. **Vocabulary:** Precise legal language, but without the Academy's formality — she learned from practice, not from books. Her precision is earned, not performed.
2. **Sentence length:** Medium and controlled. Never rushes. Never trails off.
3. **Contractions/formality:** Formal in business, warmer in private — but the warmth is strategic. She knows when to deploy it.
4. **Verbal tics:** Refers to contracts by their ratification date, not their subject. "The Bellwright agreement of twelve years past." Makes it sound historical. Settled.
5. **Question ratio:** Very low. She states. When she asks a question, it's because she already knows the answer and wants you to say it aloud.
6. **Interruption:** Never. Interrupting is imprecise. She waits, then responds to what you actually said, not what you meant to say.
7. **Metaphor domain:** Architecture and structure. "That argument doesn't bear weight." "You're building on sand." "The foundation was sound."
8. **Directness:** Completely direct about business. Her indirection is reserved for things she doesn't want on the record.

**Example lines:**
- *"The contract is valid. It was sung in proper form. The registrar confirmed it. If you have a legal objection, I suggest you engage a song-broker and file it through the appropriate channel."*
- *"Grudges expire, Eddan. Contracts don't."* (Clipped. The warmth in her voice is worse than coldness.)
- *"Your son is well-treated. He is also very good at his work. That's not a coincidence."*

#### Physical Appearance

Small and upright. Gray hair worn close to her head. Hands that are still, always — she learned early that visible nervousness is a negotiating disadvantage. She dresses in the Merchant Tier's solid colors, always navy or dark green, never anything that could be read as ostentatious. She wears no jewelry except a single tuned bronze ring on her left hand that rings at G when struck. She strikes it against surfaces when she's thinking. People who know her know to wait.

#### Physical Habits and Unconscious Tells

- **Strikes the ring** against whatever surface is nearest when working through a problem. A table. A windowsill. Her own thumbnail.
- **Breathes before she speaks** in any high-stakes conversation — a full, controlled breath, the kind they teach in contract singing. Old habit from her broker days.
- **Does not look at the door** when someone enters. She knows who it is before they arrive.

#### Secrets

1. **The written document.** She holds a pre-tonal-system written document that claims Corda family co-custodianship of the original bells. It is legally unenforceable. She has never tried to enforce it. She uses it as pressure — the threat of raising the question is enough.
2. **She has read the journals.** Not the originals — a copy, made by Aldric's son before the originals were sealed. She has known what the harmonic in the Civic Pitch means for thirty years. She built the Corda empire partly around the knowledge that the Bellwright family would do anything to prevent that information from becoming public.

### RECTOR SUVAINE
**Age:** 58 | **Role:** Institutional antagonist, Academy Chancellor

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** Thirty years ago, Suvaine was a junior Court Singer who witnessed a verdict she knew was wrong — a merchant convicted on a lame chord, a binding that should have been void. She raised the objection. The senior Court Singer overruled her. The merchant served three years. Suvaine spent the next decade becoming the person who could overrule the senior Court Singer. She succeeded. She has not thought about the merchant since.

**Wound:** She saw the system fail and decided the answer was to control the system more completely. She has confused her own judgment with institutional integrity.

**Lie:** *The Academy's stability is the same as Cantamura's stability. Protecting one protects the other.*

**Want:** Keep Volume Four's blank pages blank — because filling them would require acknowledging the system's gaps, which would open every contested verdict of the last forty years to review.

**Need:** Understand that the blank pages are not a problem to be managed but a question to be answered — and that she is not the right person to answer it.

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 7 | She moves to protect the institution constantly, preemptively. She is proactive in defense. |
| **Likability** | 5 | She is not cruel. She genuinely believes she is protecting Cantamura. The tragedy is that she's not wrong that the system needs protecting — she's wrong about what protecting it requires. |
| **Competence** | 8 | Brilliant legal mind. The best Court Singer of her generation. Her competence is the reason her blind spot is so dangerous. |

#### Speech Pattern

Formal, measured, never imprecise. She speaks the way a verdict sounds — structured, final, with the resolution built into the opening phrase. She does not raise her voice. She does not need to. Her sentences are long and complete; she never leaves a thought unfinished, because unfinished thoughts can be misquoted.

**Example lines:**
- *"The interval exists. Your interpretation of it does not appear in any volume of the Codex. We deal in codified intervals here."*
- *"I have been Chancellor for nineteen years. I have seen twelve attempts to open Volume Four. Each time, the attempt was motivated by genuine concern and would have produced genuine chaos. I take no pleasure in saying no. I say it anyway."*

### TORVALD HESS
**Age:** 63 | **Role:** Compact leader, outer-district ally

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** Torvald was a dye-merchant whose trade partnership collapsed when his business partner ratified a codicil amendment Torvald couldn't hear — they were standing near the Fade Line, the acoustic boundary was ambiguous, and the Academy ruled the amendment valid. He lost the business. He spent the next twenty years building the Compact.

**Wound:** He was failed by a system he'd trusted, and he is still angry about it — but the anger has been refined into something useful. Mostly.

**Lie:** *The Compact can replace the system. Folk intervals and community witnesses are enough.*

**Want:** Build the Compact's parallel legal infrastructure until it's undeniable — force the Academy to recognize it.

**Need:** Accept that the Compact cannot replace the system, only pressure it — and that a fourteen-year-old boy who hears things no one else can hear might be more useful than thirty years of tally-songs.

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 8 | He has been building something for twenty years. He does not wait. |
| **Likability** | 8 | Gruff, specific, funny in a blunt way that has no patience for ceremony. He treats Cass like a person, not a child. |
| **Competence** | 7 | Exceptional at negotiation and community organizing. Weaker at the formal legal theory that would let him challenge the Academy on its own ground. |

#### Speech Pattern

Loud (he's from the Tanner's Quarter, where people talk at normal volume and regard inner-city whispers as pretension). Direct. He says the thing. He uses folk interval terminology that the Academy doesn't recognize, which he does partly because it's what he knows and partly because it annoys Academy people. Partial deafness means he sometimes misses the first word of a sentence and picks up mid-phrase, which gives his responses a slightly delayed, considered quality.

**Example lines:**
- *"Forty years I've been settling disputes with folk intervals. Academy says they don't count. Ask the people whose disputes got settled."*
- *"You hear something they can't measure. Good. Unmeasurable things make lawyers nervous. Use it."*
- *"Tell me what you heard, boy. Exact pitch. Forget the Academy words for it — just the sound."* (Cuts through formality, goes straight to the raw data. Respects Cass's ear, not his credentials.)

#### Physical Appearance

Large, going soft at the edges. Partially deaf in his right ear from decades near dye-vat machinery. He cups his left ear toward speakers without thinking about it. His hands are stained blue-green from thirty years of dye work, permanent now, no amount of washing shifts it. He finds this funny.

### LENNE ASHVALE
**Age:** 14 | **Role:** Cass's Academy peer, friend

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** Lenne's mother Vella Ashvale is a licensed Court Singer — one of the four currently operating, and one of the two not retained by the House of Corda. Lenne has grown up watching her mother compose verdicts she disagrees with, sung in perfect form, legally binding, final. She has learned to perform certainty she doesn't feel.

**Wound:** She has been trained to trust the form over the content — if the interval is correct, the verdict is correct. She is beginning to suspect this is not the same thing.

**Lie:** *If I'm technically perfect, I can't be responsible for what the technique produces.*

**Want:** Top marks at the Academy. A career as a Court Singer like her mother.

**Need:** Develop her own judgment, separate from her training — learn to hear what the intervals don't say.

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 5 | She works hard and achieves within the system. She does not push against it. Yet. |
| **Likability** | 7 | Precise, occasionally prickly, genuinely curious. She asks questions that make Cass uncomfortable in ways that are good for him. |
| **Competence** | 9 | The best student in their year. She knows it and doesn't perform modesty about it, which Cass finds refreshing. |

#### Speech Pattern

Formal, technically precise, with a dry edge she's inherited from watching legal proceedings since childhood. She uses correct Academy terminology even in casual conversation — not to show off, but because it's her native language. She asks more questions than she makes statements, which is unusual for an Academy student trained to assert.

**Example lines:**
- *"The Codex says a codicil must resolve within the same breath. Has anyone measured how long a trained singer's breath actually lasts?"* (Questioning the premises, not the conclusions.)
- *"My mother sang the Merchant Quarter verdict last spring. I watched. The form was perfect. I've been thinking about it since."* (Precise observation leading to unfinished thought — she trails to silence, not to a point.)
- *"Describe what you heard. The actual sound. Forget what it means for a moment."* (Direct request, cutting through interpretation to get at the sensory data.)

### DAV SORN
**Age:** 34 | **Role:** Court Singer, divided loyalties

#### Ghost / Wound / Want / Need / Lie

**Ghost:** Dav is one of the two Court Singers in Cantamura not retained by the House of Corda — the other being Vella Ashvale. He got the position by being exceptional and by being willing to take cases the Corda-affiliated singers declined. He has spent eight years telling himself this independence is meaningful.

**Wound:** He has composed verdicts he knew were shaped by the Corda singers' availability — cases where his clients couldn't afford the Corda singers' fees, where the outcome reflected the power imbalance rather than the merits. He has sung these verdicts in perfect form. The form was always correct.

**Lie:** *My independence from Corda means my verdicts are independent.*

**Want:** Keep his position and his reputation as an independent Court Singer.

**Need:** Use that position for something that costs him something.

#### Three Sliders

| Slider | Score | Justification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Proactivity** | 4 | He reacts. He is waiting for a situation that forces his hand, and he is not sure he will make the right choice when it arrives. |
| **Likability** | 6 | He knows what he should do. The reader knows he knows. This makes him sympathetic and frustrating in equal measure. |
| **Competence** | 9 | Technically the finest Court Singer in the city. The Corda singers are good; Dav is better. This is why they've never moved to eliminate him — he's too useful as a legitimizing presence. |

#### Speech Pattern

Careful. He chooses words the way he chooses intervals — for what they commit him to. He speaks in qualifications. "In my assessment." "Based on available evidence." "It would be premature to conclude." He is not evasive — he is a man who has spent eight years composing legally binding verdicts and knows exactly what words cost.

**Example lines:**
- *"In my assessment, the original ratification contains what could, if your account is reliable, be characterized as a lame chord. The word 'if' is load-bearing."* (Hedged to the point of suffocation, but precise underneath it.)
- *"I've sung forty-three verdicts in the last two years. I've been satisfied with eleven of them."* (The rare moment where his precision becomes self-indictment.)

#### Physical Appearance

Slight, with the upright posture of someone trained in breath control. He keeps his voice in condition the way a craftsman keeps tools — regular exercises, careful hydration, no shouting.
